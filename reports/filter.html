MedFact — Filter Results

Each node is a PubMed paper coloured by its truthfulness verdict. Gold stars are works that refuted it; red edges mark refutations.

What this shows: the filter deciding whether each paper is truthful. Papers arrive as PubMed XML. For each paper, the filter first pulls out the claims it makes. It then gathers evidence on those claims from two places: the paper's own correction and retraction links inside the XML, and a search of trusted medical literature. It reads how each study it gathered relates to the claim, whether it disproves it, supports it, or neither. Each box is a paper, coloured by the verdict that results, and a star marks a study that disproved it. The verdict tells downstream training to keep, down-weight, or drop the paper, and it comes from the gathered evidence, not from a model's own opinion.
