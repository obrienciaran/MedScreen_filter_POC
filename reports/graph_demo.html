MedFact — Evidence Graph

A test of evidence retrieval. Each box is a medical claim, and the lines link it to the studies the search brought back, coloured by whether each study supports or disproves the claim.

What this shows: whether an automated search can find the studies that disprove a wrong medical claim. That is the one thing the whole filter depends on. Each dark blue box is a claim the medical field later overturned. Each green box is a claim that still holds today, kept as a control. For every overturned claim, our hand-checked dataset already records which study overturned it, so we know the right answer in advance. The system then searches the medical literature and we check what it brings back. A plain blue dot is a study the search found. A blue dot ringed in gold is the known disproving study, found by the search (a success). An orange triangle is that known disproving study when the search failed to find it (a miss, and the gap this test measures). The lines show how each study relates to the claim: red disproves it, green supports it, grey is neither. A language model is used in only two narrow places, to read each claim from its text and to judge whether a study disproves or supports it; it does not decide which papers are kept or dropped, and the claims and their known disproving studies here were chosen by hand.
